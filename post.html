Here we go!

Yup, getting ready now...

Posted by Sarah Wilson on 27 February 2018

All opinions are my own, this is based off my experience, and information may be out dated or just actually wrong. I’ll set up a contact form at some point and you can correct me if it upsets you enough.

I’ve tried to keep each topic brief. If you want to learn more, real information, may I suggest you visit google.

Speech

I think I took speech for granted until I studied it.

For example, how many vowels are there? 5? According to the Latin alphabet there are, but if in terms of the sounds we make when we talk, it’s more like 20, and there’s more like 24 consonants.

With some accents the sounds are distinct to others. We just kind of map them on the ones that we use.

An examples of this is with the Liverpudlian accent, the "k" sound in "back" isn't the same k as other British accents, but your brain thinks it is, but just weird version of it.

It’s why I think the brain is cool. You can hear a bunch of sounds you’ve never heard in your life, from someone with a completely alien accent to yours and in a fraction of a second figure out that yes, they also think this weather is rubbish.

Steering

I've been lucky enough to drive, sail and fly things. The land steering is the most simple, it’s all 2D. The stuff (ground) underneath you isn’t moving and the direction you aim to go in, is normally the direction you end up going.

At sea, the stuff (water) underneath you is doing its own thing dragging you about with it and you’re probably being pushed by wind. The direction you want to go in has to take into account these things.

In the air, it all becomes a bit insane. You are being pushed around by a variable and invisible force. To top it off, the amount you tilt up or down (pitch) affects your speed and if you really get it wrong you can stall the thing and end up hurling towards the ground.
